## Course Description

**ISM 6405: Advanced Business Analytics** is a graduate-level course in the College of Business at Florida Atlantic University. It is designed for students in the **Master of Science in Management Information Systems**, **MBA**, and related programs who seek deep, applied expertise in data-driven decision-making.

This course moves decisively beyond descriptive statistics and introductory visualization. It is organized around three convictions:

1. **Analytics is a strategic capability**, not a technical department. The most powerful analysts are those who understand business problems deeply enough to translate them into analytical questions — and analytical answers back into business action.

2. **AI has permanently changed the analyst's job**. Tools powered by large language models, generative AI, and intelligent automation do not replace the analyst — they radically amplify what a skilled analyst can accomplish. This course trains you to wield that amplification deliberately and responsibly.

3. **Real learning requires real doing**. Every major concept in this course is reinforced through hands-on work with industry-standard and cutting-edge tools. You will not just read about machine learning pipelines — you will build them. You will not just discuss AI assistants — you will deploy them.

### Topics Covered

The course is organized into seven thematic modules, each building on the last:

| Module | Title | Theme |
|--------|-------|--------|
| 1 | Foundations of Advanced Analytics | Data thinking in the AI era |
| 2 | Predictive Modeling and Machine Learning | From patterns to predictions |
| 3 | Natural Language Processing and Text Analytics | Unlocking unstructured data |
| 4 | AI-Augmented Analytics Workflows | Human + machine collaboration |
| 5 | Data Visualization and Storytelling | Communicating insight to power |
| 6 | Conversational AI and Voice Analytics | The next interface layer |
| 7 | Ethics, Governance, and the Future of Analytics | Leading responsibly |

### Credit Hours and Format

- **Credits:** 3 graduate credit hours
- **Format:** Hybrid (in-person sessions + asynchronous lab work)
- **Prerequisites:** ISM 6136 or equivalent graduate-level statistics/data management coursework, or instructor approval

## The AI Disruption: Why This Course Exists

```{admonition} The Paradigm Shift
:class: warning

In 2020, a skilled business analyst needed months to build a predictive model, required a data scientist partner to deploy it, and spent weeks writing the Python or R code to clean messy enterprise data.

In 2024, a skilled business analyst equipped with the right AI tools can prototype that same model in an afternoon, interrogate it through natural language, deploy it as a conversational interface, and generate a polished executive summary — all without writing a single line of code from scratch.

**This is not a threat to analysts. It is a superpower — but only for those who understand what they are doing and why.**
```

The business analytics landscape has undergone a transformation so rapid that most traditional curricula have not caught up. Consider what has changed:

**Generative AI has democratized code.** Tools like Claude and GitHub Copilot mean that the barrier to writing analytical code is no longer syntax knowledge — it is *problem formulation*. The analyst who can clearly articulate what question needs to be answered will outperform the analyst who can only write loops.

**Large language models have made unstructured data tractable at scale.** Customer reviews, clinical notes, regulatory filings, earnings call transcripts, social media — data that once required expensive human annotation can now be processed, summarized, and analyzed at enterprise scale. This opens entire new classes of analytical problems for business strategists.

**Conversational interfaces have changed how analytics is consumed.** Dashboards are giving way to intelligent agents. Executives increasingly interact with their data through conversation, not through BI tools. The analyst of 2025 must understand how to design and evaluate these interfaces.

**Autonomous agents are beginning to conduct analysis independently.** Platforms like Manus represent an emerging category of AI that can be tasked with complex, multi-step analytical work and return results with minimal human handholding. This raises new questions about oversight, validation, and accountability.

This course was designed specifically to navigate this disruption — to give you both the technical fluency and the professional judgment to thrive in an environment that is changing faster than any syllabus can fully anticipate.

This course uses a carefully curated stack of tools that reflects the actual landscape of professional analytics in the AI era. Below is your toolkit for the semester:

---

### ☁️ Google Colab — *Your Cloud Analytics Workbench*

**What it is:** A free, cloud-hosted Jupyter notebook environment from Google that requires no local installation and runs Python with GPU/TPU support.

**Why we use it:** Colab eliminates the "it works on my machine" problem entirely. Every student begins every lab from an identical environment. It also integrates seamlessly with Google Drive for collaborative work and supports all major Python data science libraries out of the box.

**What you'll do with it:**
- Execute all course lab notebooks directly in your browser
- Build and train machine learning models without local compute constraints
- Share and collaborate on live notebooks with teammates in real time
- Integrate with Gemini AI for code assistance within the notebook environment

**Getting started:** [colab.research.google.com](https://colab.research.google.com) — sign in with your FAU Google account.

```{seealso}
All lab notebooks are distributed through the course Google Drive folder and linked directly from each chapter. You never need to download anything.
```

---

### 🎧 NotebookLM — *Your AI-Powered Research Companion*

**What it is:** Google's AI-native research and synthesis tool that allows you to upload source documents and have deep, grounded conversations with your own curated knowledge base.

**Why we use it:** NotebookLM represents a new paradigm in how analysts engage with literature, reports, and data documentation. Rather than skimming dozens of PDFs, you can interrogate them intelligently — and, critically, every response is grounded in *your* uploaded sources with citations, reducing hallucination risk dramatically.

**What you'll do with it:**
- Build a personal knowledge base from course readings, industry reports, and research papers
- Generate structured study guides and concept summaries from complex academic texts
- Conduct comparative analysis across multiple documents simultaneously
- Create audio overviews to review course material during your commute (yes, really)
- Ground your analytical project research in verified source material

**Pro tip from Dr. Lee:** Upload all seven module readings at the start of the semester. Ask NotebookLM to identify connections across modules before we've covered them in class. You'll arrive at every session with better questions.

---

### 🤖 Manus — *Your Autonomous Analytics Agent*

**What it is:** Manus is an autonomous AI agent platform capable of executing complex, multi-step tasks — including data analysis, research, code execution, and report generation — with minimal step-by-step human guidance.

**Why we use it:** Manus represents the frontier of what is sometimes called "agentic AI" — systems that can plan, act, and iterate toward a goal rather than simply responding to a single prompt. Understanding how to task, monitor, and validate autonomous agents is an increasingly critical skill for senior analysts and analytics managers.

**What you'll do with it:**
- Delegate complex data preparation and exploratory analysis tasks to an autonomous agent
- Evaluate agent-generated analytical reports for accuracy and business relevance
- Design task specifications (sometimes called "briefs" or "prompts") that produce reliable, high-quality autonomous work
- Critically assess where autonomous agents fail, hallucinate, or make inappropriate analytical choices

```{warning}
**Academic Integrity Notice:** When using Manus (and all AI tools in this course), you are required to document your prompts, the tool's outputs, and your own critical evaluation of those outputs. Submitting AI-generated work without this documentation — and without your own analytical judgment layered on top — is a violation of FAU's academic integrity policy. AI is your collaborator, not your ghostwriter.
```

---

### 🧠 Claude (Anthropic) — *Your AI Reasoning and Coding Partner*

**What it is:** Claude is a large language model developed by Anthropic, designed with a particular emphasis on helpfulness, harmlessness, and honest reasoning. It is available through [claude.ai](https://claude.ai) and via API.

**Why we use it:** Among the frontier LLMs available today, Claude demonstrates particular strengths in extended analytical reasoning, nuanced code generation, and careful handling of complex, multi-constraint problems — precisely the tasks that arise in graduate-level business analytics. Its "Constitutional AI" design philosophy also makes it a useful subject of study when we examine AI ethics in Module 7.

**What you'll do with it:**
- Generate, debug, and explain Python code for analytical tasks in Colab
- Receive detailed explanations of machine learning concepts tailored to your background
- Draft and iterate on analytical narratives and executive summaries
- Explore "what if" analytical scenarios through conversation
- Conduct model interrogation: asking Claude to explain, critique, or stress-test your own analytical choices

**A note on working with Claude effectively:** Claude is most powerful when you give it context. Don't just ask "write me a logistic regression." Tell it your dataset, your business question, your audience, your constraints. The quality of your output is a direct function of the quality of your input — a principle that applies to every AI tool in this course, and to analytics itself.

---

### 🎙️ Vapi — *Your Voice AI Development Platform*

**What it is:** Vapi is a developer platform for building, testing, and deploying voice-based AI agents — conversational systems that interact with users through spoken natural language, in real time.

**Why we use it:** Voice is the next primary interface for analytics consumption. Executives and frontline workers alike are increasingly interacting with business intelligence systems through voice — asking questions, requesting updates, and receiving spoken summaries rather than navigating dashboards. Vapi allows us to prototype these systems at a level of sophistication that was previously accessible only to specialized engineering teams.

**What you'll do with it:**
- Design and deploy a voice-enabled business analytics assistant as part of your final project
- Understand the architecture of real-time voice AI: transcription, LLM reasoning, text-to-speech, and latency management
- Evaluate the user experience of voice interfaces for analytics delivery
- Explore use cases in customer service analytics, field sales intelligence, and executive briefing systems

```{admonition} Module 6 Highlight

The Module 6 lab is one of the most distinctive experiences in this course. You will build a functional voice assistant that can answer questions about a business dataset — in real time, through natural speech. Students consistently describe this as the moment the course "clicked" for them in terms of understanding where analytics is going.
```

## Course Philosophy: Human + Machine

```{epigraph}
The analyst who uses AI thoughtlessly will be outcompeted by the analyst who uses AI thoughtfully. But both will outcompete the analyst who refuses to engage with AI at all.

-- Dr. Ernesto Lee, ISM 6405 Course Introduction
```

This course takes a clear position: **AI augments exceptional analysts; it does not create them from average ones.**

The temptation with powerful AI tools is to shortcut the hard work of understanding — to get an answer without building the capacity to evaluate that answer. This course is specifically designed to resist that temptation. Every AI-assisted task in this course is paired with a critical evaluation component. Every generated output must be interrogated. Every recommendation must be justified in terms that demonstrate your understanding, not the machine's.

At the same time, this course fully embraces the reality that the analyst who can fluidly collaborate with AI tools — who can prompt precisely, evaluate critically, iterate efficiently, and communicate compellingly — is dramatically more capable than the analyst who cannot. We are not training you for the analytics job of 2010. We are training you for the analytics landscape of 2025 and beyond.

The balance we strike in ISM 6405 is this: **AI as amplifier, human as author.**

## Assessment Overview

```{note}
Full grading rubrics, submission guidelines, and due dates are available on the course Canvas site. What follows is a conceptual overview of how your learning will be assessed.
```

| Assessment Component | Weight | Description |
|---------------------|--------|-------------|
| Lab Notebooks (8 labs) | 30% | Completed Colab notebooks with code, outputs, and written reflections |
| AI Reflection Journal | 10% | Ongoing documentation of your AI tool use, prompt strategies, and critical evaluations |
| Midterm Business Case | 20% | Individual analysis of a structured business dataset with predictive modeling and presentation |
| Module Discussion Posts | 10% | Weekly contributions to Canvas discussions on readings and current events |
| Final Capstone Project | 30% | Team-based, end-to-end analytics project using the full course tool stack |

### The Capstone Project

The final project is the centerpiece of ISM 6405. Teams of three to four students will:

1. **Identify a real business problem** with a public or partnered dataset
2. **Build an analytical solution** that incorporates predictive modeling and at least one NLP component
3. **Create a data story** in Tableau or Looker Studio for an executive audience
4. **Deploy a conversational or voice interface** to the analytical findings using Vapi
5. **Document their AI collaboration workflow** including all tools used, prompts crafted, and outputs evaluated
6. **Present to a panel** that includes both Dr. Lee and, where possible, an industry practitioner guest

Past projects have analyzed healthcare appointment no-show rates, predicted customer churn for regional banks, assessed sentiment in FAU student course reviews, and built voice assistants for small business financial monitoring.
